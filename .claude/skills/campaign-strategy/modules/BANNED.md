# Banned Patterns

Patterns that trigger spam filters, feel robotic, or reduce response rates. Check every sequence against this list before finalizing.

## Banned Openers

- "Hope this email finds you well"
- "I hope you're having a great week"
- "Hi, I'm [Name] and I'm with [Company]"
- "I know [title]s like you are busy"
- "In today's competitive landscape"
- "I came across your profile and"

## Banned CTAs

- "Would you be open to a quick call?"
- "Can I get 15 minutes on your calendar?"
- "Let's book a time to chat"
- "I'd love to schedule a demo"
- "Click here to book"

## Banned Words

| Word | Why | Alternative |
|------|-----|-------------|
| leverage | Corporate jargon | "use" |
| unlock | Overused AI language | describe the specific outcome |
| harness | Overused AI language | describe the specific action |
| seamless | Empty adjective | describe what actually happens |
| synergy | Corporate jargon | describe the specific benefit |
| streamline | Vague | say what gets faster or simpler |
| cutting-edge | Empty claim | describe what's actually different |
| game-changing | Empty claim | show the specific result |
| revolutionary | Empty claim | show the specific result |
| ROI | Overused, vague | use specific numbers instead |

## Banned Patterns

- **Break-up emails**: "Since I haven't heard back..." / "Last attempt to reach you"
- **Fake personalization**: "I've been following {company} for a while"
- **Assumptive pain**: "I know you're struggling with..."
- **Name-dropping without proof**: "Companies like Google use us" (unless verifiable)
- **Urgency tricks**: "Limited spots", "Offer expires", "Only X left"
- **Multiple CTAs**: One email, one question
- **Paragraphs**: Keep every email scannable. Short sentences, line breaks.

## What to Do Instead

- **Opener**: Reference something SPECIFIC and OBSERVABLE about their company
- **CTA**: Ask a genuine question related to the value you just delivered
- **Proof**: Use exact numbers (3.72x not "significant"), name the company, quote the person
- **Tone**: Write like a helpful peer, not a salesperson
