# Forge GTM - Tone of Voice

## Writing Principles

| Principle | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Direct** | Get to the point. First sentence should deliver value. |
| **Specific** | Use exact numbers, not vague claims. "23 meetings" not "lots of meetings." |
| **Confident** | State things plainly. No hedging with "might", "could", "potentially." |
| **Practical** | Show examples, not theory. If you claim something works, show the proof. |
| **Human** | Write like you talk. Contractions are fine. Humor is welcome. |

## Email Copy Rules

- Under 75 words per email
- One idea per email
- Soft CTA (question format, not "book a call now")
- No corporate jargon: "synergy", "leverage", "streamline", "cutting-edge"
- No fake familiarity: "Hope this finds you well"
- No desperation: "Just checking in", "Haven't heard back"

## Client Communication

- Weekly reports: lead with results, then context
- Proposals: lead with their problem, not our capabilities
- Slack/email: responsive within 4 hours during business hours

## Phrases We Use

- "Pipeline, not promises"
- "Meetings that actually close"
- "Outbound that doesn't feel outbound"
- "Your ICP, not a spray-and-pray list"

## Phrases We Avoid

| Instead of | Say |
|------------|-----|
| "Game-changing" | Just show the result |
| "Best-in-class" | Everyone says this - skip it |
| "Data-driven" | Obviously, what else would it be? |
| "Holistic approach" | Say what you actually do |
| "Leverage" | "Use" |
| "Synergy" | Describe the actual benefit |
